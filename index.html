Breathing

Slow, deep diaphragmatic breathing shifts your autonomic balance toward the parasympathetic (rest-and-digest) system, which lowers heart rate and increases heart-rate variability - physiological signs of reduced stress.

Meditation

Regular mindfulness or focused-attention meditation trains the brains attention systems (including the anterior cingulate and prefrontal regions) to detect and redirect distraction, strengthening sustained attention and reducing mind-wandering.

Walking

A brief walk boosts blood flow to the brain and reduces stress hormones, which helps calm racing thoughts and lift your mood.
